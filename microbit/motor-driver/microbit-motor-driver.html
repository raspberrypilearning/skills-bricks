Motor Driver

Output Motor

What does it do?

A motor driver board lets your micro:bit control motors that need more power than a pin can supply.

The micro:bit sends low-power signals and the driver switches the larger motor current from its own battery.

With a micro:bit, you set direction with digital pins and speed with a PWM (analog) pin.

JavaScript

// motor forward at about 70% speed
pins.analogWritePin(AnalogPin.P0, 700)
pins.digitalWritePin(DigitalPin.P1, 0)
Power the motors from the driver board's own battery, never from the micro:bit. Many driver boards have a MakeCode extension with ready-made motor blocks. Use pin0.write_analog() to set the motor speed.
