ARDUINO SKILL BRICKS — USE-CASE ILLUSTRATION PROMPTS
=====================================================

These are image-generation prompts for the two "use case" scenes on each Arduino
card. There is one prompt per use case (44 in total). Each suggests a filename —
drop the finished PNG into the matching card folder and the scene will be wired in.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────
GLOBAL STYLE  (prepend to every prompt, or set as your style preset)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Technical illustration with a rugged, make-do maker aesthetic — an improvised
hardware build like something thrown together on a hackerspace workbench. Always
feature an exposed Arduino Uno, a breadboard, and visible colour-coded jumper
wires; enclosures made from scrap cardboard, plywood, jar lids and tape; small
hand-written paper labels. Flat, slightly desaturated and worn colour palette,
clean confident linework, soft shading, a little grime and wear. Show ONLY the
device and the scene being described — no people, no hands, no faces. Neutral
background or a plain workbench surface. Square framing, slight 3/4 / isometric
angle.

Naming: filenames are kebab-case of the use-case title. Place each in
arduino/<component>/ and they will replace the matching placeholder.
